Dear Feeld Team,

I want to be straightforward about why I'm applying to Feeld specifically, and not just any senior research role: I think the questions you're asking — how do people navigate desire, identity, connection, and relationship structures in a digital space — are some of the most genuinely interesting research problems in consumer tech right now. My research background spans both ends of the sensitivity spectrum. At ABIM, I designed annotation and evaluation frameworks for clinical AI models trained on physician-patient conversations — a domain where nuance, dignity, and ethical care are non-negotiable. I built protocols that protected patient data while enabling meaningful behavioral analysis at scale. At UrbanFit, I conducted usability testing and contextual interviews for the Stories By Children platform, working with families and children in a context that required careful, trust-based facilitation. These experiences gave me a deep respect for what it means to research around identity, vulnerability, and intimacy — and the methodological discipline to do it responsibly.

On the mixed-methods side, I've built research programs that span qualitative discovery and quantitative validation. At John Deere, I ran end-to-end research across 6+ product teams — from contextual inquiry and journey mapping through behavioral analytics and usability benchmarking — synthesizing findings across methods to generate insights that shaped quarterly roadmaps. At Capita, I designed behavioral cohorts and A/B experiments across 7 fashion brands, identifying high-value user segments and conversion patterns through statistical significance testing. At UrbanFit, I used Hotjar heatmaps and session recordings alongside usability interviews to drive a platform redesign that delivered 35% engagement lift and improved task success on the Stories By Children platform from 50% to 85%.

Feeld is building a space where people can explore who they are and what they want. That's not a product you can research with surface-level methods — it requires depth, trust, and a genuine belief that understanding human behavior in this domain matters. I bring that belief, along with the methodological toolkit to act on it. I'd love to be part of the team asking these questions.
