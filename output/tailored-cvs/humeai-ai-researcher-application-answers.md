### Why Hume AI / Why this role

Hume AI's core thesis — that AI optimized for human well-being requires learning from human expression, not just human text — is one I find both scientifically compelling and ethically important. Reinforcement learning from human expression (RLHE) is a fundamentally different approach to alignment, and it puts the measurement of human emotional and expressive signals at the center of model training. That measurement problem is one I've been working on in a parallel domain: at ABIM, I build NLP models to detect behavioral and communicative patterns in clinical audio/text data, design annotation pipelines for subjective human judgments, and build inter-rater reliability frameworks for labeling human expression. I have 2+ years contributing to large-scale NLP model training with text and behavioral datasets, strong PyTorch experience, and production-quality Python skills — exactly what Hume's AI Researcher role requires. Beyond the technical fit, I'm drawn to The Hume Initiative and the ethical guidelines work: I want to build AI that treats human expression as something to honor, not exploit.
