### Why GitLab / Why this role

GitLab's approach to AI is different from most: it's being built into an open-core platform that millions of developers rely on daily, with all the transparency and community accountability that comes with open-source. Researching AI features for GitLab means studying a user base that is technical, opinionated, and deeply embedded in their workflows — which makes for richer, harder, more interesting research questions. The UX Researcher AI role sits at the center of how GitLab Duo and AI-assisted development tools actually land with users. From GitLab's handbook-driven culture to its async-first remote model, the way GitLab operates also matches how I work: I'm used to making research visible, documented, and reusable — not locked in decks that get lost. At John Deere, I built a PM-UX Playbook and self-serve research infrastructure specifically so insights would outlive individual projects. That's the culture GitLab's research team values.
