## Cover Letter

Dear YOU.com Hiring Team,

I'm excited to apply for the Senior UX Researcher role at YOU.com. As an AI-powered search engine, you sit at the frontier of one of the most compelling UX challenges of our era: helping users understand, trust, and act on AI-generated information. That intersection of AI output and human interpretation is precisely where my research expertise lives.

At ABIM, I led the design and execution of a clinical NLP evaluation study involving 985 physician-patient conversations and 3,500+ annotated clinical records. My work required me to study how people — both physicians and patients — interpret and respond to AI-generated assessments of clinical communication quality. I built annotation taxonomies, established inter-rater reliability protocols, and designed bias-detection frameworks using fine-tuned transformers (ClinicalBERT, RoBERTa). This experience gave me a rigorous, hands-on understanding of how AI models can misrepresent nuanced human behavior — and how to surface those gaps through research. Translating that to search: I understand how AI-generated results can mislead users, create false confidence, or omit critical context, and I know how to design studies that reveal those failure modes before they ship.

Prior to ABIM, I spent two years at John Deere as the sole UX researcher on the India team — effectively building a research practice from scratch. I ran mixed-methods programs across 6+ product teams, from contextual inquiry and usability testing to behavioral analytics and journey mapping. I also authored a PM-UX Playbook that increased PM UX literacy by 80%, because I believe research only creates value when it reaches decision-makers in formats they can act on. I designed a self-serve survey platform used by 250+ products, reducing setup time by 60% — demonstrating that I think not just about research insights but about research infrastructure at scale.

YOU.com is building the search experience people will use to navigate an AI-saturated information landscape. Getting that trust layer right — understanding how users form mental models of AI-curated results, when they verify versus accept, and where the interface can scaffold better decisions — requires a researcher who can work at the intersection of behavioral science, AI output evaluation, and product thinking. That's where I operate best. I'd love to bring this perspective to your team.
