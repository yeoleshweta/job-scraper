## Cover Letter

Dear Grow Therapy Research Team,

Grow Therapy is solving one of the most important access problems in healthcare — connecting people who need mental health support with providers who can actually take their insurance. Getting that experience right for both patients and therapists requires deep, empathetic research. That's why this role caught my attention.

My background spans two domains that directly serve this work. Currently at ABIM, I study clinical communication patterns in physician-patient interactions — working with sensitive healthcare data, applying IRB-adjacent ethical protocols, and generating insights from unstructured clinical conversations. I understand research involving vulnerable populations, the care required in recruitment and consent, and the analytical complexity of healthcare user journeys. Before this, at John Deere, I was the sole UX researcher for the India team: running mixed-methods studies across 6+ product teams, building research infrastructure from scratch, and writing the PM-UX Playbook that raised PM UX literacy by 80%.

That last piece — coaching and enabling non-researchers to own and drive research — is the part of this role I'm most excited about. Grow Therapy's JD explicitly describes a researcher who "coaches and supports Product Designers and PMs as they own and drive team-centric research projects." This is exactly what I built at John Deere: structured enablement, research templates, and design-thinking workshops that made research a team sport rather than a specialist handoff. On a three-sided marketplace (patients, therapists, insurers), that kind of distributed research capacity becomes essential to moving fast without losing the nuance.

I bring mixed-methods depth (contextual inquiry, diary studies, usability testing, behavioral analytics, A/B testing), research-ops experience, and a sensitivity to the high-stakes emotional context that healthcare research demands. I'd welcome the opportunity to contribute to Grow Therapy's mission. My portfolio is at shwetasharma.tech.

Sincerely,
Shweta Sharma
