Dear Rula Hiring Team,

I am applying for the Lead User Experience Researcher role at Rula. Rula's mission — making mental healthcare accessible by connecting people with the right therapist and reducing the friction between need and care — is one I find genuinely compelling. I bring what a Lead UXR role at a healthcare platform requires: deep experience building research practices from scratch, the ability to synthesize complex qualitative and quantitative data into product strategy, and direct exposure to the sensitivities of clinical and health-adjacent product contexts.

At John Deere, I served as the sole UX researcher across six product teams — the equivalent of a founding researcher mandate — responsible for establishing research practices, methodologies, and infrastructure where none previously existed. I built the PM-UX Playbook (increasing PM UX literacy by 80%), designed and launched a self-serve survey platform now used across 250+ products, and ran the full Double Diamond process from discovery through validated recommendations for every major initiative I supported. The "Lead" framing in this role resonates with that experience: it is not just about executing studies, but about building the research function's credibility and influence within a product organization.

At ABIM, I work in a domain where the stakes of getting the product wrong mirror what Rula faces: clinical AI systems that, if poorly designed, could affect physician behavior and ultimately patient outcomes. I design annotation frameworks for clinical communication behaviors, build NLP bias-detection pipelines on 3,500+ records, and develop synthetic data workflows that protect patient privacy while enabling AI training. The clinical sensitivity and ethical rigor required in that environment — knowing what questions not to ask, what data not to collect, how to protect vulnerable populations — is directly transferable to researching a mental healthcare platform where users are often in vulnerable states. I understand that designing a good intake flow for someone seeking therapy is not a neutral UX problem.

At UrbanFit, I led research for the Stories By Children platform — a product serving families — and drove task success from 50% to 85% through iterative usability testing and contextual interviews with parents and children. At Capita, I built behavioral cohorts and ran statistically significant A/B experiments that drove 11% conversion lifts on high-traffic e-commerce platforms. I know how to pair qualitative insight with behavioral data, move quickly when the situation calls for directional research, and apply rigor when the decision is high-stakes. Rula needs both modes. I am excited about the opportunity to apply this range to a mission I believe in, and I would welcome the chance to discuss how I can help Rula build the research practice this stage of growth requires.
