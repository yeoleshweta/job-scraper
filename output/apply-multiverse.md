## Cover Letter

Dear Multiverse Hiring Team,

I'm applying for the Senior UX Researcher, Client Experience role because Multiverse's mission — building a world where skills and potential matter more than credentials — is one I find genuinely compelling. And the challenge of this specific role is one I've lived: researching how enterprise stakeholders think, decide, and learn.

At John Deere, I was the sole UX researcher on a team serving 6+ product squads across enterprise software. My stakeholders were product managers, engineers, and business leaders — experienced professionals with strong mental models and limited time. To succeed in that context, I learned to make research legible to non-researchers, not just surface insights but change how teams made decisions. The outcome I'm most proud of is the PM-UX Playbook I created: a structured guide that increased PM UX literacy by 80% by translating research principles into the language and workflow of product management. That's essentially what Multiverse does for employers — it enables organizations to make more research-informed, skills-based talent decisions. I understand that mission from the inside.

The Client Experience lens of this role also resonates with my experience designing the self-serve survey platform for 250+ internal products at John Deere. That project required me to understand client needs at scale — not through one-off research sessions but through a repeatable, systematic understanding of how different product teams think about feedback and measurement. I conducted discovery interviews, mapped the journey of a survey from brief to analysis, and designed an experience that cut setup time by 60% and raised completion rates by 25%. That kind of B2B, client-facing research — understanding complex organizational workflows and translating them into product improvements — is exactly the work Multiverse's Client Experience team is doing.

I bring mixed-methods depth (usability studies, contextual inquiry, behavioral analytics, surveys, journey mapping), strong stakeholder influence skills, and a track record of building research infrastructure that outlasts any single study. I'd love to help Multiverse understand its employer-partners even more deeply, and to make research a competitive advantage in how you serve them.
