## Cover Letter

Dear BetterUp Research Team,

BetterUp's mission — helping people everywhere pursue their lives with greater clarity, purpose, and passion — is one of the most research-rich problems in human-computer interaction. Coaching at scale means understanding behavioral change, measuring outcomes that are hard to quantify, and designing AI-assisted experiences that feel human. That's exactly the intersection I work in.

I'm currently a Data Scientist Co-op at ABIM, building NLP models to detect and classify clinical communication behaviors — patterns like empathy, coaching cues, and active listening — across 3,500+ physician-patient interactions. I design annotation workflows, build inter-rater reliability protocols, and generate behavioral insights from unstructured conversation data. The skills are directly translatable: BetterUp's AI team is operationalizing coaching behaviors in similar ways, and I understand both the machine learning layer and the human-judgment layer underneath it.

Before pivoting to AI, I built UX research practice from scratch at John Deere. As the sole researcher for the India team, I drove mixed-methods studies across 6+ product teams — contextual inquiry, usability testing, behavioral analytics, journey mapping — and synthesized findings into roadmap decisions. I also wrote the PM-UX Playbook that increased PM UX literacy by 80%, essentially coaching non-researchers to be research-informed partners. That coaching-adjacent mandate maps directly to BetterUp's work, and to what research culture-building inside a small-but-growing AI team requires.

I'd welcome the chance to discuss how my background in both rigorous qualitative methods and AI/NLP systems can serve BetterUp's research mission. My portfolio is at shwetasharma.tech.

Sincerely,
